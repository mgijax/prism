Load a new J#:
Enter the J# in the text field at the top left and hit Return (or tab out of the field). The first image for the J# is opened automatically. If the J# has no figures in the database (or cannot be found), an error message appears. If a figure record exists, but has no associated image (JPEG file), a "No image available" message appears.
See a given figure and its panes:
Click on the figure label in the table on the left. The image for the figure is displayed. If pane coordinates have already been defined, the corresponding overlays are also displayed. If there is no corresponding image, "No image available" is displayed. (Eventually, will provide the ability to upload image files and associate them with figures.)
Draw a region:
Drag the mouse over the desired section of the image.
Split regions into two:
Select the region(s) to split. Then Option-click (hold down the Option (or Alt) key while clicking). All selected regions will be split along a horizontal line though the clicked location. (Regions not intersected by the hirizontal are unaffected.) To split along the vertical, also hold down the Shift key (Option-Shift-click).
Select region(s):
Click on it. To select multiple regions, hold the shift key down while clicking. Also: If an image pane has coordinates, then clicking on the pane's row in the table will select the region in the image. Also: clicking the button "SelectAll" will select all regions.
Delete region(s):
Select the region(s) to delete and click the "Delete" button (or type the corresp. keyboard shortcut). Panes that were currently associated with the regions will be dis-associated (and display a "?" for coordinates).
Associate a pane with a region:
Option 1: Drag the pane label from the table and drop onto the desired region. Option 2: click "OnePane" to associate a single pane with the entire image area. Option 3: Click "ClickAssign" (note: the button stays depressed), and then click the regions in order. Panes will be assigned in order. You may click on a different panelabel (in the table) to start/continue from a different point. When you are done, click "ClickAssign" again, to reset the button. Option 4: Click "GridAssign". Autoassigns all panes in order to defined regions in a left-to-right, top-to-bottom order. Option 4 works better when you have defined regions by splitting rather than by drawing freehand.
Mouse actions:
clickClicking on a region selects it.
shift-clickSelects multiple regions.
dragDraws a new region.
option-clickSplits all selected regions along a horizontal line through the click point.
option-shift-clickSplits all selected regions along a vertical line through the click point.
Tip: on a fresh image (no regions yet) option-click or option-shift-click on the image will automatically create a covering region and then do the split.
